About

More About Me

Doctor in High Energy Physics, I develop software for future large collider projects, such as the International Linear Collider (ILC). I develop algorithms to identify particle collision products in large detectors and understand the physics behind each collision.

I'm currently developping Marlin, our C++ data analysis framework, towards a usage on multi-core machines.

My work in High Energy Physics had started during my PhD thesis during which I developed a novel particle flow reconstruction algorithm approach based on the tree-like development of electromagnetic and hadronic showers observed in our detectors. This was indeed a conbination of HEP research and software development.

Since then I have this passion for software and in particular in the HEP research field. My next research topics where very much targeted on HEP computing, including HEP application development, full-stack software development / deployment and HEP event processing framework development. The latter is currently my main topic; I'm refactoring and extending our lovely event processing framework Marlin towards a multi-threaded version called MarlinMT.
